Action Potential

Remember that action potential starts when there's a stimulus to the body. This can be touch, heat, or anything that evokes a stimulation in the body.

Resting Potential Sodium (Na+) and Potassuim (K+) channels are closed. Negative and positive ions are flowing in or out of the membrane.
Stimulus The stimulus occurs and causes the sodium channels to open. Na+ flows through the channel and depolarizes the membrane, triggering an action potential (once reaching threshold).
Depolarization More sodium channels open and the inside of the membrane becomes positive. (as opposed to negative like before)
Repolarization Sodium channels close while Potassium channels open, which Potassium flow out, causing the inside of the cell negative again.
Undershoot The Potassium channel eventually closes and the inactivation gates of the Sodium channels open which makes the membrane return to its resting state.
