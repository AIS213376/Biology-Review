Mouth - You chew the food with saliva to break it down to startch. This process is called ingestion.

Pharynx - Food goes through this to esophagus

Epiglottis - Folds over the glottis to stop liquid and food from entering the trachea when you swallow food so that food goes right to the esophagus.

Esophagus - Send food to stomach; Larynx makes the opening of esophagus larger

Stomach - Food, water, and stomach acid are mixed together to produce thick liquid called chyme. This process is called digestion. Mucus helps protect stomach from acidic fluids.

Duodenum - First part of small intestine. Chyme is broken down into small particles.

Jejunum - Second part of small intestine. Its innermost layer is formed by villi which absorbs nutrients from ingested food (Absorption).

Ileum - Third part of small intestine. It also has villi. Food particles are broken down into nutritional components.

Liver - Process fats and proteins from digested food

Gallbladder - Stores and concentrates bile

Pancreas - Produce pancreatic juice that includes enzymes, bicarbonate, and alkaline. Pancreatic juice helps digesting fat, proteins, and carbohydrates.

Cecum - Receives undigested food material from the small intestine, egestion starts.

Rectum - Receive stool from the colon which is located in large instestine.
