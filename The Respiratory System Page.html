Animal Physiology
The Respiratory System

Definition:

The Respiratory System is a biological system consisting of specific organs and structures used for the process of respiration in an organism

Fuctions:

The function of the Respiratory System is to supply the blood with oxygen in order for the blood to deliver oxygen to all part of the body.

1. Nasal Cavity: The nose

2. Mouth: entrance / beginning of the digestive tract

3. Pharynx (or throat): permits the passage of swallowed solids and liquids into the esophagus, and conducts air to and from the trachea, or windpipe, during respiration.

4. Larynx (vocal cord): manipulates pitch and volume. 

5. Trachea (windpipe): connects the larynx to the bronchi and allows air to pass through the neck and into the thorax.

6. Lungs: help oxygen from the air we breathe enter red blood cells. 

7./8. (right/left) Bronchus: a passag

9. Bronchiole: the passageways by which air passes through the nose or mouth to the alveoli (air sacs) of the lungs

10. Alveoli: Gas exchange of oxygen and carbon dioxide takes place

11. Diaphragm: The muscle that separates the chest (thoracic) cavity from the abdomen.
